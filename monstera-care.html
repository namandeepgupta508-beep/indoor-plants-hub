Monstera Deliciosa Care: Your Guide to the Swiss Cheese Plant

The Monstera Deliciosa, with its iconic split leaves, is a stunning statement plant. Contrary to its dramatic looks, it's surprisingly easy to care for if you know its few simple needs.

Light: The Key to Fenestrations

To get those beautiful split leaves (called fenestrations), your Monstera needs plenty of bright, indirect light.

  • Ideal: Near an east or west-facing window.
  • Tolerates: Medium light, but growth will be slower and leaves may not split.
  • Avoid: Hot, direct afternoon sun which can scorch the leaves.

How to Water Your Monstera

Monsteras like to dry out a bit between waterings.

  • Check the soil: Water thoroughly when the top 2-3 inches of soil feel dry.
  • Seasonal Change: Water more frequently in spring/summer (growth season) and reduce watering in fall/winter.
  • Sign of Overwatering: Yellowing leaves, particularly the lower ones.
  • Sign of Underwatering: Drooping leaves, brown crispy edges.

Soil, Potting, and Support

Use a well-draining, peat-based potting mix. A mix designed for aroids is perfect. As your Monstera grows, it will develop aerial roots and want to climb. Provide a moss pole or stake for support.
